The Program
What is the CMS ACCESS Program?
A 10-year CMS Innovation Center initiative that expands technology-enabled care to Medicare beneficiaries with chronic conditions — reimbursing organizations based on measurable health improvements, not visit volume.
  • Outcome Aligned Payments (OAPs) — organizations are paid when patient health measurably improves: blood pressure, weight, lipids, hemoglobin A1c
  • WHOOP is not the provider — Baylor clinicians remain the primary care authority; WHOOP extends care between visits through continuous monitoring and behavioral guidance
  • Highly scalable — one referral workflow reaches thousands of eligible patients with no proportional increase in clinical burden
  • Funding follows outcomes — this is not fee-for-service; reimbursement is tied directly to documented clinical improvement across the enrolled population

Clinical Reporting
What Baylor sees — and when
Clinical Reports
  • Structured summaries sent monthly (or preferred cadence) to the patient's PCP, endocrinologist, or cardiologist
  • Delivered to EHR inbox — no new tooling or workflow required from providers
  • Includes: BP trends, activity, sleep, lab uploads, adherence, and flagged changes
  • Real-time escalation alerts when clinically relevant thresholds are crossed
Health System Dashboards
  • Dedicated Baylor dashboard — population-level view of all enrolled patients
  • Metrics at program, cohort, and individual level: BP control rates, engagement, outcome trajectory
  • Supports ACO quality reporting and population health workflows
  • Data available via secure API; EHR and data stream specifications to be defined jointly

The Referral Model
Two paths to enrollment — and what Baylor receives
Path 1 — Provider-Initiated
  • Baylor shares a list of eligible Medicare patients fitting the eCKM profile
  • WHOOP contacts candidates directly, obtains consent, and manages enrollment — no clinical team involvement required
Path 2 — Patient-Initiated
  • Patients discover WHOOP ACCESS through in-office materials or a provider mention during a routine visit
  • Patient self-enrolls through the WHOOP app — minimal provider workflow beyond the initial touchpoint
What Baylor Receives
  • ~$100 per enrolled patient per year in Medicare care coordination payments — billed monthly through existing co-management codes
  • Enrolled patients arrive with continuous data already documented — reducing time spent reviewing BP and lifestyle metrics at appointments
  • Population health resources can be redeployed toward higher-complexity cases
  • Better-controlled chronic conditions reduce downstream ED utilization and hospitalizations — meaningful for ACOs with exposure to total cost of care
At 100,000 enrolled patients: ~$10M in annual care coordination payments
