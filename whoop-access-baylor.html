The Program
What is the CMS ACCESS Program?
ACCESS is a 10-year CMS Innovation Center initiative that expands technology-enabled care to Medicare beneficiaries managing chronic conditions — reimbursing organizations based on measurable health outcomes, not visit volume.
  • Outcome Aligned Payments (OAPs) — organizations are paid when patient health measurably improves: blood pressure, weight, lipids, hemoglobin A1c
  • WHOOP is not the provider — Baylor clinicians remain the primary care authority; WHOOP extends care between visits and handles day-to-day engagement
  • Highly scalable — the model is designed for population-level deployment; one referral workflow can reach thousands of eligible patients
  • Funding follows outcomes — this is not a fee-for-service model; reimbursement is tied directly to documented clinical improvement
  • Focused on the early cardio-kidney-metabolic (eCKM) track: hypertension, prediabetes, obesity, dyslipidemia

Strategic Intent
Why WHOOP is in this program
WHOOP is not primarily in ACCESS to generate revenue. This is the highest-visibility opportunity in the industry to demonstrate that continuous wearable data — combined with behavioral science and AI — can produce meaningful clinical outcomes in mid-to-moderate chronic disease.
  • The best device in the world deserves the hardest test — ACCESS puts WHOOP alongside Medicare's most complex chronic disease population and asks for measurable results
  • Lifestyle and medicine together — WHOOP's model is not a replacement for medication or clinical care; it closes the gap between appointments through continuous monitoring and behavioral guidance
  • Mid-to-moderate conditions are the right fit — patients who are metabolically at risk but not yet in crisis are exactly where sustained behavioral change and early intervention matter most
  • Millions of members worldwide, ships to 56 countries, 14-day battery life — the infrastructure to scale this nationally already exists
  • Proving clinical outcomes in a CMS-sanctioned model establishes WHOOP as the standard for health system wearable partnerships globally

The Member Experience
From referral to enrolled member — what the patient experiences
01
Eligibility & Enrollment
Patient fills out a questionnaire in the WHOOP app to confirm eCKM eligibility. WHOOP verifies Medicare status and enrolls the member.
02
Kit Delivery & Onboarding
WHOOP ships a kit — device and best-in-class blood pressure cuff. Member is onboarded digitally with dedicated member support throughout the program.
03
Continuous Monitoring & Data
Daily blood pressure readings, lab result uploads, sleep, recovery, and activity tracked continuously. Data is visible within the WHOOP platform.
04
Behavior Change Program
Condition-specific in-app programs: diabetic diet plans, medication adherence nudges, CMS track exercise goals. WHOOP AI coach generates personalized weekly plans targeting the member's exact conditions.
05
Clinical Escalation
Baylor providers are engaged only when clinically relevant — structured alerts via DIRECT to EHR inbox when thresholds are crossed or meaningful trend changes occur.

Clinical Reporting
What Baylor sees — and when
Clinical Reports
  • Structured clinical summaries sent on a monthly cadence (or preferred interval) directly to the patient's care team
  • Delivered to PCP, endocrinologist, or cardiologist via DIRECT to EHR inbox — no new tooling required
  • Report includes: BP trends, activity levels, sleep performance, lab uploads, adherence metrics, and flagged changes
  • Escalation alerts sent in real time when clinically relevant thresholds are crossed
  • Providers review data on their own schedule — no routine workflow burden is added to the clinical team
Health System Dashboards
  • WHOOP can deploy a dedicated Baylor dashboard — population-level view of all enrolled patients across the network
  • Metrics visible at program, cohort, and individual level: BP control rates, engagement scores, outcome trajectory
  • Supports ACO quality reporting and population health management workflows
  • Data available via secure API endpoints for integration into Baylor's existing analytics infrastructure
  • Data streams and EHR integration specifics — including HL7 FHIR compatibility — to be defined jointly as part of the partnership agreement

The Referral Model
Two paths to enrollment — and what Baylor earns from each
Path 1 — Provider-Initiated
  • Baylor provides a list of eligible Medicare patients who fit the eCKM profile
  • WHOOP reaches out to candidates directly, obtains consent, and manages enrollment — Baylor's clinical team is not involved in the process
  • Flexible on what "good candidate" looks like — WHOOP can work from ICD codes, population health lists, or care coordinator referrals
Path 2 — Patient-Initiated
  • Patients encounter WHOOP ACCESS through in-office flyers, EHR portals, or a provider recommendation during a routine visit
  • Patient self-enrolls through the WHOOP app — no provider workflow required beyond the initial touchpoint
What Baylor Receives
  • ~$100 per enrolled patient per year in Medicare care coordination payments — billed monthly through existing co-management codes, no new billing infrastructure required
  • Staff efficiency gains — PCPs currently spend appointment time reviewing BP readings and lifestyle adherence; enrolled patients arrive with 12 months of continuous data already documented
  • Population health nurses and care coordinators can be redeployed from manual outreach to higher-complexity cases
  • Better-controlled chronic conditions reduce downstream ED visits and hospitalizations — meaningful for ACOs and risk-bearing entities with financial exposure to total cost of care
  • At 100,000 enrolled patients across Baylor's network: ~$10M in annual care coordination payments
