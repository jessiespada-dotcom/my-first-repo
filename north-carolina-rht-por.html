NC ROOTS Hub Lead Awards Are Being Made NOW — This Is the Window
The NC ROOTS Hub Lead RFA (2026-NCROOTS) closed April 2, 2026 — 11 days ago. NC DHHS is evaluating applications right now. Up to 6 Hub Lead organizations (one per NC Medicaid region) will be selected in May–June 2026. These organizations become the technology procurement decision-makers for their entire regional network. WHOOP's strategy: engage the Hub Lead applicants before awards are announced so WHOOP is on their vendor list from day one.
Why Hub Leads Matter — One Deal = Regional Network Scale
Each Hub Lead is the "programmatic and fiduciary lead" for their NC Medicaid region. They manage the partner network, coordinate technology deployment, and distribute sub-awards to local health organizations. One Hub Lead partnership = reach across all facilities in their region. NC has 85 rural counties served by 400+ rural health facilities. Six Hub Leads collectively cover the entire state. This is NC's equivalent of MO's RCN structure — the leverage point is at the Hub Lead level, not individual hospitals.

Six Core Initiatives — WHOOP Fit
# Initiative WHOOP Fit Notes
6
Technology & Innovation
 Primary AI deployment, health information sharing, digital literacy initiatives. Consumer health technology for patient self-management is explicitly in scope. WHOOP = consumer-facing biometric monitoring platform. No vendor named. Direct lane.
2
Prevention & Chronic Disease Management
 Primary Diabetes, hypertension, cancer screening, maternal health, nutrition/food-as-medicine. Continuous biometric monitoring (HRV, resting HR, SpO2, sleep) directly supports chronic disease outcomes measurement. WHOOP's core clinical use case.
1
Community-Rooted Care Networks (NC ROOTS Hubs)
 Secondary 6 regional hub networks connecting medical, behavioral, and social services. Each Hub Lead deploys technology tools across their partner network. Hub Lead partnership = network-wide deployment. WHOOP as the consumer health data layer for the hub structure.
3
Behavioral Health Expansion
 Secondary Mental health clinics, mobile crisis teams, school-based programs, primary care integration. Sleep quality and HRV are validated biomarkers for behavioral health outcomes. WHOOP's sleep tracking data is a secondary fit here.
4
Workforce Development
 Secondary Rural residency programs, provider incentives, innovative career pathways. WHOOP Unite = direct workforce retention tool for rural NC providers. ~36 Critical Access Hospitals statewide with high provider turnover risk.
5
Financial Sustainability
 Not Fit Transition to value-based payment models. Payment model innovation — no technology vendor entry point in BP1.
