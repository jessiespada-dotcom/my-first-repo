Training Zones — know where every player stands in the load cycle
The Three Zones
  • Overreaching — Building fitness, strain exceeds recovery. Productive short-term; monitor closely to avoid breakdown
  • Maintaining — Steady-state load. Player is adapting and holding readiness across the schedule
  • Tapering — Load reduction to peak for competition. Critical before playoff series — and a flag if it runs too long
Marlins Application
  • Structure the 162-game load scientifically — prevent non-functional overreaching in the back half of the season
  • Flag players tapering too long between starts and losing fitness quietly
  • Give coaching staff and trainers one shared, objective view — no more guesswork on who is ready to play

Why Now
162 games. 30 cities. One season to get it right.
The MLB schedule is among the most grueling in professional sports. Sleep deprivation from night games, cross-timezone travel, and cumulative fatigue are the real opponents over 6 months — and they're invisible without data.
  • WHOOP quantifies the exact cost of a road trip — in HRV drop, sleep debt, and recovery score
