The Nebraska Opportunity — Named Remote Monitoring, Active Workforce Window, and a Technology Fund with No Vendor

Nebraska received $218.5M under the CMS Rural Health Transformation Program — the 8th-largest state award nationally — and structured its program with a level of initiative specificity that is unusual in the national portfolio. Most states build broad technology or innovation categories. Nebraska named its eHealth initiative explicitly: Initiative 4 (eHealth and Mobile Care) funds chronic disease management and remote patient monitoring — not "health technology broadly," but the specific use case that WHOOP occupies. This is as direct a mandate as any state in the portfolio has written for a product like WHOOP. No vendor has been named. No RFP has been released.

Nebraska's Initiative 7 (Rural Health Technology Catalyst Fund) is the dedicated procurement vehicle for the program — a competitive grant fund for consumer health technology deployment in rural settings. No vendor selected. No solicitation released. This is the same structural opportunity as Alaska's Spark Technology Fund — a designated pool where WHOOP can win on its own merits as a consumer health technology rather than being named as a line item inside a larger program.

The third Nebraska opportunity operates on a different timeline. The Rural Health Workforce Incentive Program opens for applications on April 15, 2026 — two days from today. This program awards $7,500–$75,000/year for 5-year rural service commitments to physicians, nurses, and allied health professionals in rural Nebraska. Facilities participating in this program are currently thinking about what makes their recruiting package competitive against larger urban systems. WHOOP Unite is that differentiator. A rural CAH that is about to commit a physician to a 5-year rural service incentive can offer WHOOP Unite as part of the benefits package — provider wellness technology that helps rural physicians manage the physical and cognitive demands of high-load rural practice. The urgency is real: these facilities are making those decisions now, not in a future procurement cycle.

Below are three GTM plans for Nebraska, structured around the near-term, medium-term, and strategic plays — with a 30/60/90 day action plan and opening email to the DHHS RHTP program office.

Initiative 7 (Rural Health Technology Catalyst Fund) is Nebraska's dedicated competitive procurement vehicle for consumer health technology in rural settings. Neither initiative has a vendor named. Neither has released an RFP or solicitation. The pre-market window is the moment to introduce WHOOP to DHHS as the consumer wearable monitoring platform before either vendor selection process is defined. Contact DHHS.RHTP@nebraska.gov — introduce WHOOP as the continuous remote monitoring platform for chronic disease management, explain WHOOP's fit for both Initiative 4 and Initiative 7, and ask about the timeline for vendor selection in these two initiatives. Register as a vendor at das.nebraska.gov/materiel/vendor-information.html and monitor DHHS grants/contract opportunities pages for solicitation releases. The single most important action in Nebraska is getting a pre-RFP conversation with DHHS before the Initiative 4 and Initiative 7 vendor ecosystem is defined.

Contact Nebraska's 41 CAHs and the network of FQHCs and RHCs directly.
