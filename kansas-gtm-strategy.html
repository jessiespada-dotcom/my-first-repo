The Kansas Opportunity — Active Funding, Unique Infrastructure

Kansas received $221.9M under the CMS Rural Health Transformation Program — the 6th-largest state award nationally — and structured it around a mechanism unlike any other state in the program: the Evidence-Based Practices (EBP) Program. Rather than funding hospitals through subawards that must be competitively solicited, Kansas routes $33M through its existing EBP infrastructure — a pay-for-reporting system that 200+ rural Kansas providers are already enrolled in and actively receiving payments from as of Budget Period 1.

The critical fact: the EBP Program's Frequently Asked Questions document explicitly states that providers can invest infrastructure payments in "technology or data infrastructure." This is WHOOP's direct funding lane — not a future NOFO, not a pending RFP, but an active program where Kansas rural providers have money in hand and are making technology investment decisions right now. The spend deadline is October 30, 2026. The window is open today.

The EBP infrastructure is operated by the UKHS Care Collaborative Association (CCA) — the intermediary organization that manages EBP program enrollment, conducts on-site provider visits, and works directly with the 200+ enrolled providers to help them meet EBP criteria and invest program funds effectively. The CCA is the single-highest-leverage Kansas contact in the entire program: one CCA endorsement of WHOOP as the technology infrastructure tool creates a referral pathway to every enrolled provider simultaneously.

Kansas also has five initiatives and 24 programs deploying across its remaining award — including technology programs in Initiatives 1 and 5 that explicitly name continuous health insights and wearable health technology as priorities. Those programs are still in development, but the CCA relationship and the direct EBP provider conversations being built now position WHOOP for every future solicitation KDHE writes for Years 2–5.

Below are three GTM plans for Kansas — evaluated for speed, deal size, and probability — with a 30/60/90 day action plan and opening email to the CCA.
