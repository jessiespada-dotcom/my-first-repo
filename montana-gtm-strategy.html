The Montana Opportunity

Montana received $233.5M — 4th largest nationally — to transform rural healthcare across 51 counties over 5 years. Montana DPHHS administers the program with a centralized RFP model: ~8 competitive procurements and ~5 grant programs, all through eMACS (bids.mt.gov), all by September 2026.

Montana's structure has no parallel in Texas, Alaska, or California. There are no individual hospital subawards. No LOI-funded organizations to cold-call. Every dollar flows through state contracts — which means every WHOOP deal in Montana flows through either (a) subcontractor positioning with a primary contractor, or (b) a direct state-level technology RFP.

Two RFPs are live right now. The CoE Implementation contract ($105.5M, due May 15) is the central operator for the entire program — managing technology subcontractors, population health interventions, and facility modernization across all of rural Montana. The firms bidding this contract are building proposals today. WHOOP needs to be named in one of them before May 15. Initiative 5 (deploy modern health care technologies) is WHOOP's direct lane — the RFP hasn't dropped yet, but when it does, WHOOP responds as a primary vendor. Three plans below.

CoE Implementation Subcontractor · DPHHS-RFP-2026-0670R

The Approach

The $105.5M CoE Implementation contract goes to a firm that manages all of Montana's rural health transformation operations — subcontractors, technology deployments, population health interventions, and facility modernization. Whoever wins this contract will bring in technology vendors to support their scope. WHOOP positions as that technology vendor NOW — before proposals are submitted — by contacting the firms most likely to bid this contract and offering to be their named population health monitoring subcontractor.

Key Actions
  • Identify the top 5 firms likely bidding DPHHS-RFP-2026-0670R — Guidehouse, Manatt Health, Health Management Associates, Public Consulting Group, Leavitt Partners, Deloitte, BerryDunn, Wipfli — healthcare implementation consultants that bid state health transformation contracts
  • Contact their BD or partnerships teams this week — not next week. Proposals are being built now.
  • Frame WHOOP as: "We want to be your named technology partner for population health monitoring and consumer health tools in your CoE Implementation proposal"
  • Download and read the full CoE Implementation Scope of Services from eMACS — confirm the specific technology and population health language in the RFP, then map WHOOP directly to it
  • Build a one-page WHOOP subcontractor brief — maps WHOOP's capabilities to the CoE Implementation scope, ready to hand to any bidder's proposal team in 24 hours
Why It Works

One subcontractor position in the CoE Implementation contract reaches every rural facility in Montana. WHOOP doesn't need to close 51 counties worth of individual deals — the primary contractor distributes WHOOP across the program. And firms bidding a $105.5M state contract WANT credible technology subcontractors named in their proposals. It makes their bid stronger.

Plan B — Direct Lane
Initiative 5 Technology RFP · Future Wave
Direct Initiative 5 Technology Vendor
When DPHHS releases the Initiative 5 "Deploy Modern Health Care Technologies" RFP, WHOOP responds directly as the primary technology vendor — no intermediary
The Approach

Initiative 5 is Montana's technology initiative — "Deploy modern health care technologies to guide rural health interventions." This is WHOOP's cleanest, most direct lane in Montana. No subcontractor positioning. No intermediary. WHOOP submits a proposal responding to the Initiative 5 RFP as a primary vendor and competes for a state-level technology contract. The preparation work starts now: obtain the project narrative, confirm what Initiative 5 specifically describes as target technologies, build a Montana-specific WHOOP technology brief aligned to the scope, and be ready to submit the moment the RFP drops.

Key Actions
  • Email HHSRuralHealthTransformation@mt.gov to request the Initiative 5 project narrative and timeline for the technology RFP release
  • Monitor ruralhealth.mt.gov weekly — Initiative 5 technology RFP posting will appear here first
  • Register in eMACS with commodity code 2620 — this triggers automatic notification when Initiative 5 RFP posts
  • Once the project narrative is in hand, map WHOOP's capabilities to the exact technology language Montana used — this becomes the core of the proposal response
  • Engage WHOOP's BD/legal team on SAM.gov registration (UEI# required for federal-funded Montana contracts) and ensure WHOOP can contract directly with a state agency
Why It Works

This is WHOOP's highest-quality Montana deal — a state-level technology contract, not a subcontractor arrangement. If Initiative 5 names consumer wearables, wearable health insights, or population health technology (as Alaska and California's programs did), WHOOP is perfectly positioned to win it directly. The preparation starts now so WHOOP is ready when the RFP drops.

Plan C — Quick Win
Initiative 1 Workforce · WHOOP Unite Enterprise Motion
Rural Provider Wellness Entry
Get WHOOP Unite into rural Montana hospitals through Initiative 1 workforce wellness and resilience programs — fastest path to signed paper using the existing enterprise motion
The Approach

Initiative 1 workforce retention and upskilling includes "time-bound relocation assistance and wellness and resilience programs for HCPs in rural communities" as a funded activity. Rural Montana providers taking service commitments in isolated frontier communities face extreme conditions — geographic isolation, harsh climate, limited access to wellness resources. WHOOP Unite positions as the provider wellness benefit that makes rural Montana service sustainable. This uses the existing enterprise sales motion — no new government procurement cycle required. Land the staff deal, build a Montana case study, use it in the CoE Implementation subcontractor pitch and the Initiative 5 RFP response.

Key Actions
  • Identify rural Montana hospitals and critical access hospitals actively recruiting under Initiative 1 workforce programs
  • Use existing WHOOP Unite enterprise motion — this mirrors corporate wellness deals, not a government procurement
  • Frame for Montana: geographic isolation, frontier conditions, long shifts, limited wellness infrastructure access for providers
  • Target hospital HR Directors and CNOs with RHTP-funded workforce retention budgets
  • Close the first Montana deal — document as proof point for Plan A (subcontractor pitch) and Plan B (Initiative 5 direct proposal)
Why It Works

Fastest path to a signed contract in Montana. No new sales motion, no waiting for an RFP. Montana hospitals with Initiative 1 funding have money and a mandate to retain providers. WHOOP arrives as the wellness benefit that makes rural service more sustainable — the same motion that works in corporate wellness, adapted for rural frontier healthcare.

The CoE Implementation RFP ($105.5M) closes May 15, 2026 at 2:00 PM MDT. That is 33 days from today. The firms submitting proposals are selecting their subcontractors right now. There is no equivalent deadline in any other state in this program — Montana is the only state where a single contract decision determines technology access to the entire rural healthcare market. Missing May 15 means waiting for Wave 2 procurements later in 2026. Contact CoE Implementation bidders today.
Montana vs. Other States — What's Different
Texas: 80 individual hospital districts, direct outreach. Alaska: 1,800 LOI-funded organizations, ACF as gateway. California: RTAC state endorsement mechanism, 683+ orgs. Montana: centralized RFP model, no individual org pathway. This is harder to enter but higher leverage — one state contract covers the entire market. The CoE Implementation subcontractor position is the equivalent of California's RTAC endorsement. Win it once, reach everything.
