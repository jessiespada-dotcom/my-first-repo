The Opportunity

Kansas was awarded $221,890,000 under the CMS Rural Health Transformation Program — the sixth-largest state award nationally — to fund evidence-based care delivery, prevention, technology innovation, and workforce development across rural Kansas. The program is administered by the Kansas Department of Health and Environment (KDHE) and covers approximately 900,000 rural residents across a state that has structured its plan as 5 Initiatives and 24 discrete programs. Year 2 funding is explicitly contingent on Year 1 performance, meaning KDHE is under real pressure to show results before October 30, 2026.

The immediate and distinctive opportunity in Kansas is the Evidence-Based Practice (EBP) Program, launched March 30, 2026, and currently distributing infrastructure payments to rural hospitals and clinics. KDHE has contracted with the University of Kansas Health System Care Collaborative Association (CCA) to operate the program. Rural hospitals receive $100,000 in infrastructure payments and an additional $50,000 in performance incentives. Rural clinics, FQHCs, and CCBHCs receive $50,000 and $25,000 respectively. The EBP FAQ explicitly states that infrastructure payments can fund "investments in technology or data infrastructure to support expanded quality improvement activities." This is the direct lane for WHOOP.

The performance measures that EBP-funded hospitals and clinics must report on include: Diabetes Hospital Discharge Checklist, Heart Failure Hospital Discharge Checklist, Cardiovascular-Kidney-Metabolic (CKM) Syndrome management, STEMI/NSTEMI protocols, stroke management, and sepsis response. These are the exact chronic disease conditions that WHOOP monitors continuously — resting heart rate, heart rate variability, sleep quality, cardiovascular strain, and recovery readiness. A rural Kansas hospital that deploys WHOOP as its biometric monitoring infrastructure has a data collection layer that directly supports EBP performance measure reporting on its highest-burden patient populations. The funded hospitals and clinics are signing their Participation Agreements right now and building their technology workplans. WHOOP needs to be in those conversations before the workplan investments are finalized.

Beyond the EBP program, Kansas has programmed three technology-specific programs under Initiatives 1 and 5 that have not yet been solicited: Program 1.2 — Consumer-Facing Technologies, Program 5.1 — Remote Patient Monitoring, and Program 5.4 — Emerging Technology. These are future NOFOs — but they represent the larger, multi-year technology deployment opportunity. WHOOP is the exact product category each program was designed for. No vendor has been selected. The pre-market engagement window is open simultaneously with the EBP rollout.

CCA is the structural leverage point. CCA operates the EBP program for all eligible Kansas providers, produces all resources and tools, and conducts on-site visits with every participating hospital and clinic. Getting WHOOP endorsed by CCA as the recommended biometric monitoring technology for EBP participants means state-level reach to every funded provider simultaneously — without WHOOP needing to close each hospital individually. CCA's mandate explicitly includes helping providers make "investments in technology or data infrastructure." WHOOP is that investment.

Why WHOOP Fits — Exact Language from Source Docs
EBP FAQ — Infrastructure
"A hospital or clinic may need to hire additional staff or make investments in technology or data infrastructure to support expanded quality improvement activities." This is the exact language that authorizes WHOOP as an EBP infrastructure investment. No interpretation required — the FAQ names this category explicitly.
EBP — Performance Measures
Hospital measures: Diabetes Discharge Checklist, Heart Failure Discharge Checklist, Cardiovascular-Kidney-Metabolic Syndrome, STEMI/NSTEMI, Stroke. Clinic measures include: CKM Syndrome, Abnormal WHtR (waist-to-height ratio) with action plan, Medication Reconciliation. WHOOP continuously monitors the biometric markers — HRV, resting heart rate, sleep quality, cardiovascular strain — that underlie every one of these conditions.
Initiative 5 — Program Vision
"Rural Kansas providers should leverage technology, from telehealth and remote monitoring to data analytics and mobile health solutions, to bridge geographic gaps, enhance care delivery, and empower patients." This is Kansas's stated technology vision. WHOOP is the mobile health + remote monitoring solution this language was written for.
CCA Structure
CCA is contracted to provide "development and provision of supportive resources" and "verification of provider infrastructure investments." CCA visits every EBP-participating hospital and clinic within 12 months of attestation. A WHOOP partnership with CCA means the WHOOP recommendation reaches every provider during that on-site visit — at the moment they're deciding how to spend their infrastructure funds.
Year 2 Pressure
"Year 2 funding contingent on Year 1 performance." KDHE must demonstrate outcomes to CMS to unlock Year 2 funding. Hospitals and clinics that fail to show performance improvement on EBP measures risk Kansas losing Year 2 funding. WHOOP gives providers the continuous monitoring data they need to demonstrate exactly the outcomes that protect Year 2 funding.
