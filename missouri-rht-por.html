Q2 2026 — Remote Patient Technology Feedback Window Is Live Now
Missouri DSS has opened a Remote Patient Technology Feedback period in Q2 2026 — April through June. This is a pre-procurement market research window where DSS is actively soliciting vendor input before issuing a formal RFP. Being present in this window is how vendors get named in the subsequent solicitation. Email DSS.RHTP@dss.mo.gov now. Reference the Digital Foundation pillar and the dedicated $3M RPM allocation.
$3M Dedicated RPM Allocation — No Vendor Selected
The Year 1 fund distribution explicitly allocates $3,000,000 for RPM licensing and integration within the Digital Foundation pillar. The Digital Foundation description reads: "EHR modernization and upgrades, interoperability and Cloud hosting, remote patient monitoring integration, and ambient AI tools." No vendor has been named. This is the most specific WHOOP fit in the 9-state portfolio — the only state with a dedicated dollar line for RPM licensing.
Program Structure — 3-Tier Network Architecture
1
State — DSS / MO HealthNet Division
Central administrator. Issues competitive procurements. Sets technology standards for RCNs and Hubs. One DSS partnership = reach to all RCNs simultaneously.
2
Regional Coordinating Networks (RCNs)
Intermediate administrators. First cohort being onboarded Q2 2026. Each RCN manages technology deployment and vendor relationships for their region's community hubs.
3
Community Hubs — Local Healthcare Organizations
36 Critical Access Hospitals + Rural Health Clinics + FQHCs + CBOs. Receive funding through RCNs. Deploy consumer health tech tools to patients. Direct WHOOP Unite buyers.
