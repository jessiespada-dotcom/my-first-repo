Delaware Is Different From Every Other State in This Portfolio
Delaware is unlike the other 11 states in the RHTP playbook. There is no pre-market window. There is no waiting for solicitations to drop. There is a live RFP closing in three days — April 17 at 1PM — and the question is whether WHOOP gets named in a proposal before then.

The Play — Montana Subcontractor Model Applied to Delaware
HSS26062 is a services and operations contract — Delaware DHSS is hiring an organization to build and operate Rural Community Health Hubs in Kent and Sussex counties. WHOOP does not bid directly on this type of contract. The play is identical to Montana: get named in a prime bidder's proposal as the wearable health intelligence technology partner before April 17 submission.

A hub operator who includes WHOOP in their proposal is positioning WHOOP as the continuous health insights layer for two functions: (1) workforce wellbeing for their community health workers and hub staff, and (2) population health engagement for the rural Kent/Sussex populations the hub serves. No clinical infrastructure required. Fits a low-resource rural hub model exactly.
The Delaware Narrative

Delaware is the 12th state in the RHTP playbook — and it is the most time-sensitive by a significant margin. Every other state in the portfolio is either in a pre-market window, waiting for solicitations to drop, or operating in a multi-month engagement timeline. Delaware has a live RFP with a 72-hour window.

The RFP is HSS26062-RURALCHHUB. The contract is for an organization to build and operate Rural Community Health Hubs in Kent and Sussex counties — the two rural counties where approximately 40% of Delawareans live. Governor Meyer framed Delaware's program mission clearly: "A person's zip code should never dictate the quality of care they receive." The hub model is how Delaware operationalizes that goal — community-based care infrastructure extending health engagement beyond the clinic.

TidalHealth — a major health system serving the Maryland/Delaware border region and rural Sussex County — sent 6 people to the pre-bid meeting. That is the highest attendee count of any single organization at the pre-bid. They are almost certainly bidding as prime or anchor partner. DocGo, a national mobile health operator, sent 4 people — another strong prime bidder signal. Delaware State University (HBCU, Kent County anchor) sent 6 people as well and may be bidding as a community anchor in a consortium.

One email to Gregory Mann at TidalHealth today — pitching WHOOP as the wearable health intelligence layer in their hub proposal — could mean WHOOP is named in the submission that wins Delaware's $157M hub program. That window closes Thursday at 1PM.
