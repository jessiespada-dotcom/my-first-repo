The Q2 2026 Window — Why This Is the Most Specific Fit in the Portfolio
Every other state has RPM as an implied or likely use case. Missouri is the only state in the 9-state portfolio with a named $3M line item for "RPM licensing and integration" — and they are actively soliciting vendor input during Q2 2026 (April–June) before issuing the formal RFP. This is the pre-market window. The vendors who participate in this feedback period shape the requirements document. Miss it and you're responding to a spec written by someone else.
Three GTM Plans
Plan A
DSS State Partnership
Recommended
Respond to Q2 2026 RPM vendor feedback period. Position WHOOP as the consumer wearable health intelligence platform for the Digital Foundation pillar. One DSS relationship reaches all Regional Coordinating Networks and all 36 rural CAHs simultaneously. The $3M dedicated RPM allocation is the entry point — broadens to the $56.2M Digital Foundation over time.
Plan B
RCN Direct Engagement
High Value
Missouri's "Local Decisions. Local Results." structure gives Regional Coordinating Networks significant autonomy to select technology tools for their hubs. The first RCN cohort is being onboarded Q2 2026. Identify and contact RCN leads as they stand up — each RCN becomes an independent buying center for consumer health tools and workforce wellness.
Plan C
WHOOP Unite — 36 Missouri CAHs
Fastest Close
Missouri has ~36 Critical Access Hospitals across rural counties. Existing WHOOP Unite enterprise motion applies directly. $17.8M Workforce Expansion pillar funds retention programs — CAH administrators and HR directors recruiting for 5-year rural service commitments are active buyers. Fastest path to signed paper (14–30 days).

30 / 60 / 90 Day Action Plan
Days 1–30 — Immediate Actions
  • Email DSS.RHTP@dss.mo.gov — submit WHOOP as a consumer health technology vendor for the Digital Foundation feedback period. Reference the $3M dedicated RPM allocation explicitly.
  • Register on MissouriBUYS (missouribuys.mo.gov) — required to respond to any formal solicitation. Do this before the RFP drops.
  • Request DSS clarification on timeline: when does the Q2 RPM feedback period close? Who is leading the RFP development?
  • Begin WHOOP Unite outreach to Missouri CAH administrators — 36 critical access hospitals, rural workforce retention angle.
  • Monitor mydss.mo.gov/mhd/rural-health for procurement announcements and RCN onboarding updates.
Days 31–60 — Pre-RFP Positioning
  • Follow up with DSS if no reply — reference RPM feedback window and $3M allocation. Request a 20-min intro call.
  • Identify the first Regional Coordinating Networks being onboarded in Q2. Request RCN contact list or directory from DSS.
  • Prepare WHOOP Digital Foundation positioning brief — one-pager showing how WHOOP addresses Missouri's three objectives: Access, Health Outcomes, Sustainability.
  • Monitor Q3 2026 Social Care CIE procurement — WHOOP biometric data layer can feed into the closed-loop referral platform.
Days 61–90 — Scale & Deepen
  • Engage RCN leads directly — pitch WHOOP as the consumer health technology tool for their hub network. Leverage DSS relationship to get introduction.
  • Close first WHOOP Unite deal with a Missouri CAH — use as proof point in DSS RPM feedback and RFP response.
  • Prepare formal RFP response materials for Digital Foundation RPM solicitation (expected Q3–Q4 2026).
