Why Georgia Is Structurally Different from Every Other State in the Program

Georgia's GREAT Health Program ($218.9M, 7th-largest nationally) was built with a purpose that no other state in the national portfolio shares: it is explicitly designed to prepare rural Georgia hospitals for the AHEAD Model — the CMS Innovation Center's Achieving Healthcare Efficiency through Accountable Design program, a state-wide total cost of care (TCOC) model with hospital global budgets. Georgia is applying for AHEAD Cohort 4. If awarded, rural Georgia hospitals will transition to fixed annual global budgets by 2028–2029, with payments based on quality outcomes rather than patient volume.

Every dollar Georgia spends under GREAT Health in 2026–2027 is building the infrastructure rural hospitals need to succeed under that model. This changes the WHOOP opportunity in two critical ways. First, quality outcomes data matters enormously under global budgets — hospitals are paid for results, not encounters, and chronic disease monitoring data that demonstrates continuous improvement in cardiovascular, metabolic, and respiratory outcomes directly supports the performance metrics hospitals need to hit. WHOOP provides that data continuously, between clinic visits, from the patient's home. Second, Georgia is building a Consumer Engagement Platform for all Medicaid, Medicare, and State Health Benefit Plan members across every participating rural hospital's catchment area — with a compatibility assessment underway in 2026, implementation beginning in 2027, and a named consumer health platform integration planned for 2028. WHOOP is the biometric monitoring hardware and data layer that makes consumer engagement platforms comprehensive.

Georgia's GREAT Health Program also runs a set of active technology grant programs with procurement underway now: Workforce Retention Technology grants (first awards in 2026–2027), Telehealth Enhancement grants (first cycle application released 2026), and EMR Enhancement grants (2027). These are all active procurement pathways — not future NOFOs, but current grant programs with money being deployed. WHOOP Unite fits directly into the Workforce Retention Technology lane as the provider wellness and retention platform for rural Georgia hospitals that are actively recruiting and struggling to keep physicians and nurses in underserved rural communities.

The engagement entry point for everything in Georgia is the GREAT Health program office at Georgia DCH — greathealth@dch.ga.gov. Stuart Portman (Executive Director, Medical Assistance Plans Division) is the lead. The Georgia Hospital Association (GHA) has direct relationships with every rural hospital that has submitted a Letter of Intent to participate in AHEAD (LOI deadline was March 15, 2026 — hospitals have been identified). GHA is the secondary intro path to the full rural hospital network.

The AHEAD / Consumer Engagement Angle — WHOOP's Primary Strategic Lane
Under the AHEAD Model, rural Georgia hospitals will be paid based on quality outcomes — not patient volume. Chronic disease monitoring data becomes a revenue driver, not just a clinical tool. WHOOP generates the continuous biometric data (cardiovascular strain, sleep quality, HRV, resting heart rate, respiratory rate) that rural hospitals need to demonstrate meaningful population health improvements to CMS. Georgia's Consumer Engagement Platform — being assessed in 2026 and integrated with a named consumer health platform starting 2028 — needs biometric data input from wearable devices to be comprehensive. WHOOP is the hardware layer that feeds continuous physiological data into that platform. Getting WHOOP positioned as the biometric monitoring component within Georgia's consumer engagement ecosystem, and as the continuous outcomes data tool for AHEAD-participating hospitals, is the single highest-leverage action in Georgia.

Fastest Path to Revenue — Workforce Retention Technology Grants Are Active Now
Georgia's Workforce Retention Technology grant program is not a future NOFO — grants are being disseminated and first awards are being made in 2026 through 2027, with four additional rounds through 2030. The program explicitly funds "physician workforce retention technology" as a funded category (surgical robotic systems named as an example). WHOOP Unite as the clinical staff wellness and recovery platform is a direct fit for this category — supporting the recruitment and retention of physicians, nurses, and allied health professionals in rural Georgia communities that are competing against urban hospital systems for the same scarce provider talent. This is a sale that uses WHOOP's existing enterprise motion and does not require navigating new government procurement. It is the fastest path to a signed contract in Georgia.
