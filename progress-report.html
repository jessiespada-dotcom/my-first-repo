Executive Summary
Where We Stand
  • Identified and mapped the CMS Rural Health Transformation Program as a high-priority enterprise pipeline — $157M to $293M per state across 50 states. Actively working 12 states representing $2.69B in CMS award value with a structured outreach strategy for each.
  • Built a 12-state intelligence and tracking system covering live RFPs, prime bidder targets, ZoomInfo outreach contacts, urgency tiers, and deadlines — allowing real-time prioritization of outreach activity as new solicitations are released.
  • Delaware added to pipeline within 48 hours of opportunity identification — full attendee analysis of the Rural Community Health Hubs RFP pre-bid meeting (70+ organizations), GTM strategy built, and outreach initiated to 8 prime bidder organizations ahead of the April 17 deadline.
  • Active outreach underway in Delaware, Nebraska, and Montana. Nebraska Critical Access Hospital (CAH) outreach initiated in parallel with state agency pre-market engagement. Montana CoE prime bidder targeting underway with May 1 and May 15 deadlines approaching.
  • Positioning WHOOP as a continuous health intelligence layer in federally-funded rural health infrastructure — subcontractor and technology partner model, not direct state contract.

WHOOP Positioning — How We Are Entering These Markets
Market Entry Model Rationale States Using This Model Status
Subcontractor to Prime Bidder Get named as a technology partner in a prime bidder's proposal before RFP submission. Prime wins the contract; WHOOP is built into the implementation scope. No direct state contract required. Delaware, Montana Active Now
State Agency Pre-Market Introduce WHOOP to state health agencies before RFPs are written. Influences technical specifications in future solicitations. Positions WHOOP ahead of competitors before the market opens publicly. Nebraska, Missouri, Ohio, California In Progress
WHOOP Unite Direct Sales Direct enterprise sales to health systems, FQHCs, and CAHs that are receiving RHTP funding. These organizations are newly resourced and actively investing in technology. Workforce wellness and provider retention are the entry angle. Nebraska (CAH), Delaware (Beebe, Bayhealth), All States Active
Future Technology Solicitation Delaware and other states expected to release technology-specific RFPs in spring/summer 2026 — distinct from the current operational health hub RFPs. WHOOP is building state agency relationships now to be positioned for those solicitations. Delaware (Catalyst Fund), Nebraska (Initiative 7) Building
